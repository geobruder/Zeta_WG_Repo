### Background and Research Question

**Research question:** What county‑level social determinants of health, broadband access measures, and information‑environment indicators predict lower childhood vaccination uptake, and which counties are most at risk of under‑vaccination?

Childhood vaccination coverage varies across U.S. counties and is influenced by structural social determinants, access barriers, and the local information environment. State and county health boards require actionable, county‑level risk profiles to prioritize outreach and allocate resources. This project builds reproducible, interpretable predictive models and county risk maps to support targeted public‑health interventions.

---

### Stakeholders and Use Cases

**Primary stakeholder:** State Health Board — prioritize outreach, allocate mobile clinics, tailor messaging.  
**Secondary stakeholders:** County public‑health departments; CDC regional offices; public‑health NGOs.  
**Operational outputs:** ranked lists of high‑risk counties, county risk maps, reproducible code and data pipelines for periodic updates, and a one‑page policy brief for decision makers.

---

### Hypothesis and Prediction

**Hypothesis:** County childhood vaccination rates are associated with structural SDOH (income, education, insurance), broadband availability, and local vaccine‑hesitancy signals derived from survey data.  
**Prediction:** After adjusting for SDOH, counties with higher broadband access but elevated survey‑based vaccine‑hesitancy will show lower vaccination uptake than comparable counties with similar SDOH but lower hesitancy.

**Interpretation caveat:** Analyses are predictive and associative; results will be framed as associations useful for targeting interventions, not as causal claims.

---

### Data Sources Acquisition and Provenance

All datasets are public or accessible via documented APIs. Each retrieval will be logged with `download_date`, `source_vintage`, and checksum.

- **Outcome:** MMR completion rate for children 19–35 months — CDC VaxView county endpoints (API/CSV).  
- **Demographics and broadband subscription:** ACS 5‑year estimates (S2801, B19013, education tables) via Census API (requires free key).  
- **SDOH composites:** AHRQ county SDOH indicators (CSV).  
- **Broadband availability:** FCC Broadband Data Collection summaries and provider counts aggregated to county FIPS.  
- **Information environment:** CMU Delphi COVIDcast survey‑based vaccine hesitancy and vaccine acceptance signals retrieved via the COVIDcast API (county or proxy resolution).  
- **Validation supplements:** BRFSS county indicators or state immunization registry extracts where available.

**Merging key:** 5‑digit county FIPS. All joins include diagnostics and a reproducible join log.

### Known Data Issues and Design Choices

- **Temporal misalignment:** ACS 5‑year estimates are treated as stable structural covariates; vaccination outcomes and survey signals are aligned by month/year with explicit lag windows. Sensitivity analyses will test alternative vintages and lag choices.  
- **Survey signal coverage:** Where COVIDcast county signals are unavailable, a documented fallback hierarchy will be used: county → metro proxy → state proxy. Each record will include a `signal_source` flag.  
- **Suppressed counts:** Counties with suppressed vaccination counts will be flagged; options include aggregation to neighboring counties or county groups, or exclusion when suppression prevents meaningful inference.  
- **Ecological inference caution:** Results will be explicitly framed at the county level; no individual‑level claims will be made.

---

### Feature Engineering

- **Structural features:** ACS demographics, income, education, insurance, race/ethnicity proportions, population density.  
- **Broadband features:** pct_broadband_households; provider_density; served/unserved binary; interaction term **broadband × delphi_vax_hesitancy_3mo**.  
- **Information environment features:** delphi_vax_hesitancy_3mo (3‑month rolling average), 3‑ and 6‑month lags, and alternative COVIDcast vaccine acceptance signals.  
- **Composite indices:** PCA on SDOH features to create 2–4 interpretable components (component loadings reported); AHRQ sdoh_index used for comparison.  
- **Imputation flags:** MICE for covariates where appropriate; state medians for sparse features; imputation indicators retained.

---

### Analysis Plan and Modeling

**Primary modeling framework:** Predictive regression on continuous outcome `outcome_mmr_19_35`. Secondary classification analysis will be performed if stakeholders request operational thresholds (e.g., <90% coverage).

**Planned steps:**

1. **Data ingestion and cleaning:** reproducible scripts; cache raw API responses; harmonize FIPS; create `data_vintages.csv`; record checksums.  
2. **Exploratory data analysis:** distributions, missingness heatmaps, correlation matrices, and county maps of outcome and key predictors.  
3. **Unsupervised analyses:** PCA on SDOH features (report loadings) and k‑means clustering to identify county typologies; clusters profiled and mapped.  
4. **Baseline model:** Elastic Net regression tuned via cross‑validation for interpretability and regularization.  
5. **Advanced models:** Random Forest and XGBoost/LightGBM for predictive performance; hyperparameter tuning via nested 10‑fold CV. Feature importance and SHAP values will be used for interpretation.  
6. **Secondary classification:** Logistic or gradient‑boosted classification models if operational thresholds are required.  
7. **Validation:** 80/20 train/test split with stratification by state or urban/rural where appropriate; nested CV for tuning; subgroup fairness checks by RUCC and race/ethnicity strata. Calibration plots and decision‑curve analysis will assess practical utility.  
8. **Interpretation and visualization:** SHAP summaries, partial dependence plots, and county risk maps (static choropleth and interactive folium prototype). A one‑page policy brief will translate model outputs into actionable recommendations.

**Evaluation metrics:** RMSE, MAE, \(R^2\); calibration error; precision@k for top‑risk county identification; classification metrics if thresholded (Precision, Recall, F1, ROC‑AUC).

### Robustness, Sensitivity, and Ethical Considerations

- **Robustness checks:** alternate COVIDcast signals; county vs state/metro proxies; alternative ACS vintages; models excluding suppressed counties; alternative imputation strategies.  
- **Fairness and ethics:** subgroup performance checks; explicit limitations to avoid stigmatizing counties; county‑level aggregate analysis to minimize re‑identification risk.  
- **Transparency:** publish full codebook, data provenance, and notebooks in the GitHub repository; include a limitations section in the technical report and policy brief.

### Anticipated Pitfalls and Mitigations

- **Incomplete COVIDcast county coverage:** use documented fallback hierarchy and include `signal_source` metadata; run sensitivity checks.  
- **FCC data complexity:** implement reusable aggregation script mapping census blocks to county FIPS and cache intermediate results.  
- **Temporal misalignment:** treat ACS as structural covariates; align survey signals and outcomes with explicit lags and document vintages.  
- **Scope management:** prioritize core deliverables (predictive model, maps, brief); defer optional extensions if time constrained.
